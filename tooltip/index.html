Whose woods these are These woods don't really belong to anyone, but people can be strange. I think I know. His house is in the village though. He will not see me stopping here to watch his woods fill up with snow. When the woods begin to fill with snow, sounds become more muffled. The snow behaves as dampeners. My little horse must think it queer to stop without a farmhouse near, between the woods and frozen lake, the darkest evening of the year. He gives his harness bells Jingle bells, jingle bells, jingle all the way... a shake to ask if there is some mistake. The only other sound's the sweep of easy wind and downy flake. The woods are lovely dark and deep, but I have promises Fragile things indeed. We must nurture them. to keep, and miles to go before I sleep, and miles to go before I sleep.
